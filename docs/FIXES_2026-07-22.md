## P2-1 Fee alignment (includes a live commission regression)

- **Order commission regression fixed.** `order-service.getQuote` computed marketplace commission
  from `settings.serviceCommissionRate` (10%, which belongs to service bookings) instead of
  `defaultCommissionRate` (15%). Every marketplace order was being commissioned at 10%. Now reads
  `defaultCommissionRate`.
- **US `driverDeliveryFeeShare` 85 -> 75**, aligning the last outlier market with the 75/25 split
  used everywhere else. (Flagged: the old value carried a "per formula & examples" comment, so if
  85% was deliberate for the US market, revert this one line.)
- Customer service fee (5%) was already correct.

## P2-2 Cash settlement: WHT, tax escrow, debt cap

**A real money bug fixed.** `settleCashDelivery` debited the driver WITHOUT `allowNegative`. A driver
holding the customer's cash has a near-zero wallet, so the debit threw "insufficient balance", was
swallowed by the catch, and the platform silently lost the commission **while the merchant was still
credited** below. Cash deliveries now use the same allow-negative path as cash rides.

- New platform settings: `whtRate`, `cashDebtCap`, `vatOnCommissionRate` (seed version 4 -> 5).
  WHT is seeded 5% for **Ghana and Nigeria only**; other markets default to 0 rather than inheriting
  tax policy we were not given rules for. NG carries VAT 7.5% on commission. Debt caps are set per
  market in local currency (GHS 100, NGN 5000).
- `settleCashRide` / `settleCashDelivery` now withhold `driverGrossShare * whtRate%` into a tax
  escrow: wallet transactions tagged `type: "tax_escrow"` with the country, so remittance reporting
  is a filtered query rather than a new table. VAT on commission is recorded in settlement metadata
  for bookkeeping.
- **Debt cap**: `acceptRide` (cash rides) and `acceptDelivery` (cash orders) reject a driver whose
  wallet is at or past `-cashDebtCap` with "Clear your outstanding balance to accept cash jobs."
  Digital jobs are unaffected. A cap of 0 disables the limit.
- Shared helpers `debitCashDebt` and `withholdCashTax` in settlement-service; `canAcceptCashJob` in
  wallet-service. Admin Fee Structure gains a "Cash Settlement & Tax" card on the Rides tab.

## P2-3 Product dimensions + vehicle tier matching

- Product columns (all nullable/defaulted so existing listings keep working): `lengthIn`, `widthIn`,
  `heightIn`, `weightLb`, `isFragile`, `isPerishable`, `requiresOpenAir`.
- Seller "Add Product" collects size, weight and the three handling flags for physical goods, and
  now **requires** them on new physical listings. The backend stays lenient for old rows.
- `utils/vehicle-tier.ts` computes the minimum tier from total weight, a stacked-volume estimate and
  the longest single dimension, with handling flags escalating the tier:
  BIKE <= 10 lb / 0.5 ft3 / 18 in and no flags; CAR <= 100 lb / 10 ft3 / 48 in;
  SUV <= 300 lb / 35 ft3 or fragile or open-air; TRUCK above that.
  A cart with nothing measured falls back to CAR so we never under-dispatch a bike.
- Checkout stores `order.requiredVehicleTier`. Dispatch (`dispatchDeliveryDrivers`) now broadcasts
  only to tiers that can carry the load instead of the all-tiers union;
  `getAvailableDeliveries` filters by the driver's vehicle and returns `requiredVehicleTier` for the
  job-card badge; `acceptDelivery` re-checks so a stale list cannot claim an oversized job.
  PRIORITY ranks as car-sized.
